Transparency & Accountability

Our Methodology

How we collected, classified, and calculated healthcare industry donations to New York State legislators — and what the numbers do and don't tell you.

01

Data Source

All campaign finance data in this tool comes directly from the New York State Board of Elections (NYSBOE) Campaign Finance database, which is publicly available at elections.ny.gov. The NYSBOE requires all candidates for state office to publicly disclose their campaign contributions, including the contributor's name, employer, occupation, address, and contribution amount.

We download the bulk transaction files directly from the NYSBOE portal on a quarterly basis. These files include all disclosed contributions to active campaign committees for sitting members of the New York State Senate and Assembly.

Important note on disclosure

Campaign finance disclosures are self-reported by campaigns. While NYSBOE requires disclosure, the completeness and accuracy of employer/occupation fields depends on what contributors report to campaigns, and what campaigns report to NYSBOE. Some donations from healthcare industry sources may be undercounted if employer information was not fully disclosed.

02

How We Identify Healthcare Industry Donations

The NYSBOE data does not include a standardized industry classification for contributors. To identify donations from the healthcare and insurance industry, we apply keyword matching against the contributor's reported employer name and occupation fields.

We flag a contribution as healthcare industry-related if the contributor's employer or occupation contains any of the following terms:

insurance health plan hospital pharmaceutical pharmacy UnitedHealth Aetna Cigna Anthem BlueCross Blue Cross Emblem Excellus Humana Molina Centene Northwell Montefiore CVS Walgreens Rite Aid Express Scripts McKesson AmerisourceBergen Pfizer Merck AbbVie AstraZeneca Johnson & Johnson Sanofi Lilly Teva PhRMA healthcare health care medical center health system physician managed care PBM benefit manager
Classification approach

Highlighted terms in red are the broadest, highest-volume categories. Non-highlighted terms are specific organization names. Matching is case-insensitive. We review matches periodically to remove false positives (e.g., a "Health & Wellness" coaching business would be excluded on review).

03

How Totals Are Calculated

For each legislator, we sum all contributions that match our healthcare industry keyword criteria within the current election cycle. The total shown on each profile represents the gross amount received from healthcare and insurance industry sources — it is not adjusted for any refunds or returned contributions unless those are separately reported in the NYSBOE data.

The headline figure on the landing page is the sum of all individual legislator healthcare totals across both chambers.

Metric Definition
Total from healthcare lobby Sum of all contributions matching healthcare industry keywords, current cycle
Top 5 donors The five contributor organizations (by employer name) that gave the most to that legislator in aggregate, across all transactions
Biggest donors (landing page) Aggregated across all legislators — total given by each donor organization to any NY legislator in our dataset
Unique donor organizations Count of distinct employer names after normalization (e.g., "United Health" and "UnitedHealth Group" are merged)
04

Update Schedule

We update this tool quarterly, aligned with NYSBOE's disclosure filing deadlines. New York State requires campaign committees to file disclosure reports in January, April, July, and October of each year. We process and publish updated data within two weeks of each filing deadline.

The data vintage shown in the tool header reflects the most recent quarterly update. Data from the most recent filing period may not yet be reflected if we are still processing.

05

Limitations & What This Data Doesn't Show

This tool is designed to make a complex public dataset more accessible. It has real limitations that users should understand:

  • We only capture disclosed contributions. Independent expenditures, dark money, and other forms of political influence are not included.
  • The employer/occupation fields are self-reported and sometimes incomplete, vague, or missing — meaning some healthcare industry contributions may not be captured by keyword matching.
  • We do not distinguish between types of healthcare organizations — a community hospital and a for-profit insurer are both included. We believe the full scope of industry influence is relevant, but users should review individual donor breakdowns for context.
  • Contribution totals are not normalized for district size, campaign competitiveness, or total fundraising — a legislator from a large competitive district may raise more overall and thus show a higher raw healthcare total.
  • This data covers state-level campaign committees only. Federal PAC contributions, LLC contributions routed through holding companies, and in-kind contributions may not be fully reflected.
06

About This Project

This tool was built by the NYC-DSA Healthcare Working Group as part of our ongoing campaign for the New York Health Act — legislation that would establish universal, single-payer healthcare coverage for all New Yorkers.

We believe that transparency about the financial relationships between the healthcare industry and our elected representatives is essential to democratic accountability. This tool is not intended to imply corruption or illegal activity — all of the contributions shown here are legal and publicly disclosed. We believe New Yorkers have the right to know who is funding their lawmakers and to draw their own conclusions.

If you have questions about our methodology, spot an error, or want to get involved with the Healthcare Working Group, visit our main page or join NYC-DSA.
